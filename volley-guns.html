Volley-Guns

Indie Game Unity 6 UI/UX Team Project Coming to Steam

Overview

Volley-Guns is an independent video game that flips the sport of volleyball on its head by replacing traditional equipment with an arsenal of firearms. Players serve, spike, and block using weapons — each with unique physics and trajectory properties that fundamentally change how the ball behaves mid-air.

The game was developed collaboratively with my fellow peers from the Game Design Minor cohort at Michigan State University over the course of more than a year. It blends competitive sports mechanics with chaotic, arcade-style gameplay and is soon to be released on Steam.

My Role

As the UI/UX designer on the team, I owned the full interface experience — from the initial concept sketches through to the shipped build. My responsibilities included:

  • Designing and iterating on all menus, HUD elements, and the main menu in Figma
  • Building and implementing the UI inside Unity 6 using C# and Unity's UI Toolkit
  • Conducting playtest sessions to evaluate interface clarity and iterating on feedback
  • Establishing a consistent visual language and icon system used throughout the game
  • Collaborating cross-discipline with programmers, artists, and designers to ensure UI fit the overall vision

Design Challenges

One of the core challenges was communicating complex weapon stats (range, power, spin) in a way that felt intuitive during fast-paced gameplay. I explored several HUD layouts before settling on a minimal icon-based system that keeps the player informed without cluttering the screen.

Balancing the game's deliberately chaotic tone with a UI that still needed to be legible was an ongoing tension — the final design uses bold typography, high contrast, and exaggerated animations to match the game's energy while keeping key information clear.

Tools & Tech

  • Unity 6 / C#
  • Figma (wireframing & high-fidelity UI)
  • Unity UI Toolkit
  • Git / version control
  • Cross-discipline team (8 members)
