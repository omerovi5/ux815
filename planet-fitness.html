Planet Fitness Research Project

UX Research Figma Usability Study UI Redesign MSU

Overview

This project was a full UX research engagement conducted on the Planet Fitness mobile application as part of my Master's coursework in User Experience Design at Michigan State University. The goal was to apply a complete research methodology — from discovery through to prototyped solutions — to a real-world consumer app.

Research Process

The project followed a structured, multi-phase UX research methodology:

  • User Interviews — Conducted interviews with Planet Fitness members across different experience levels to uncover real-world pain points and mental models
  • Persona Mapping — Synthesised interview data into three distinct user personas representing the core audience segments
  • User Journey Mapping — Traced the end-to-end experience of key tasks (class booking, check-in, account management) to identify friction points
  • System Blueprinting — Mapped backend touchpoints and service interactions that impact the front-end user experience
  • Heuristic Evaluation — Evaluated the existing app against Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics and WCAG accessibility standards
  • Research Report — Compiled all findings into a written report with prioritised recommendations

Key Findings

Three critical issues emerged across multiple research methods: the class booking flow required too many steps with no clear progress indicator; the home screen suffered from poor visual hierarchy, burying high-frequency actions; and typography throughout the app fell below WCAG AA contrast requirements, creating accessibility barriers.

Outcome

Findings were translated into a high-fidelity Figma prototype that addressed all three priority issues. The redesigned class booking flow reduced steps from seven to four. The revised home screen reorganised the information hierarchy around actual usage data. All text elements were updated to meet WCAG AA contrast standards.

Tools Used

  • Figma (wireframing, prototyping, component design)
  • User interview facilitation
  • Journey mapping & persona creation
  • Heuristic evaluation (Nielsen's 10)
  • WCAG accessibility evaluation
  • Written research report
